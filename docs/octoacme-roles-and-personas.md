# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define and execute testing strategies to ensure software quality, reliability, and user acceptance. They work collaboratively with developers and product teams to validate features meet acceptance criteria and performance standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and test plans aligned with release requirements
- Develop and maintain automated and manual test cases
- Execute acceptance testing and sign-off on feature completion
- Report on quality metrics, defects, and testing coverage
- Coordinate with developers on test environment setup and data needs
- Identify and escalate blockers or quality risks

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release
- Minimize defects reaching production
- Build confidence in release quality through comprehensive testing

### Typical Communication
- Quality reviews in sprint demos and release gates
- Bug reports and test summary reports
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide architectural guidance, design oversight, and technical decision-making for projects. They ensure solutions align with system design principles, scalability requirements, and technical standards.

### Responsibilities
- Review and approve technical designs and architecture decisions
- Conduct code and design reviews
- Identify technical risks and propose solutions
- Mentor developers on technical best practices
- Ensure alignment with platform standards and existing systems
- Support estimation and technical feasibility assessments

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Reduce technical debt and system complexity
- Enable consistent engineering practices across teams

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review feedback and design critiques
- Technical spike documentation and feasibility assessments

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies and help teams work efficiently by removing impediments and promoting Agile best practices. They act as servant leaders who support team self-organization and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove team blockers and impediments
- Coach team members on Agile principles and practices
- Maintain sprint boards and ensure process adherence
- Support team capacity planning and sustainable pace
- Facilitate difficult conversations and conflict resolution

### Goals
- Enable team productivity and velocity
- Foster psychological safety and continuous improvement
- Build sustainable Agile practices across projects

### Typical Communication
- Agile ceremony facilitation and timeboxing
- Blocker identification and resolution support
- Retrospective action item tracking and follow-up

---

## Operations / DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage deployment infrastructure, production monitoring, and operational reliability. They enable seamless releases and maintain system health in production.

### Responsibilities
- Manage CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Provision and maintain infrastructure (staging, production)
- Monitor application health, logging, and observability
- Support rollback and incident response procedures
- Document runbooks and operational procedures
- Coordinate with development on deployment windows and dependencies

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, reliable deployments
- Maintain production system stability and performance
- Reduce mean time to detection and recovery (MTTD/MTTR)

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release deployment coordination
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Monitoring dashboards and operational metrics

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Sponsors provide strategic direction, funding, and executive approval for projects. They represent business priorities and ensure projects deliver aligned value.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project charter and resource allocation
- Provide strategic direction and business context
- Approve major scope changes and trade-offs
- Support escalation resolution for blockers
- Communicate project outcomes to broader organization
- Review project outcomes and lessons learned

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value
- Remove organizational barriers to delivery
- Align project outcomes with strategic objectives

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and status reviews
- Milestone approvals and go/no-go decisions
- Escalation resolution and trade-off approvals
